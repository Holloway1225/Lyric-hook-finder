What it's scoring for
Implication — does the line make the viewer feel caught, like you read a private thought of theirs?

Confession against self-interest — admitting something ugly or unflattering they'd rather not say. The "almost wants it back" move.

Specificity — a concrete, lived detail beats an abstract feeling.

Scroll-stop — it has to land cold, in the first 2 seconds, with zero context.

Benchmark (10/10): "and I still hate that part of me that almost wants it back."
